Tekoan Grammar Guide

The New Tekoan Language Grammar Guide (2026 edition) is the official grammar manual approved by the Bahnkut Tekoan Language Institute. It introduces the alphabet, pronunciation, syntax, word root system, and grammatical cases, making it easier for learners to master the Tekoan language.

Chapter I: The Basics

Alphabet

The Tekoan alphabet has 33 letters, including 8 vowels and 5 special consonants not found in English. Writing is left-to-right, using Latin script since 1957.

  • CJ = “ch”
  • G' = “gh/kh”
  • Ö = “ö/oe”
  • SJ = “sh”
  • Ü = “ü/ue”
  • ZJ = “dzh”

The "y" Buffer Vowel

The letter y is used as a buffer between vowels (e.g., iye, ayo, uyo). It is treated as a vowel in grammar, though it functions like a semi-vowel.

Vowel Groups

  • Big vowels: A, E, I, O → positive, used in prefixes, verb tenses.
  • Small vowels: Ö, U, Ü, Y → negative, used in suffixes and buffers.

Word Root System

Roots are formed by combining consonant groups (front, middle, back) with vowels. Example: masja (vehicle) = M + SJ + A.

Tonic Syllable Stress

Stress is marked with a circumflex (^). Nouns stress the first syllable, verbs the penultimate, adjectives/adverbs the last. Example: zjâ bûjan.

Syntax Change

Official syntax changed from SOV (subject-object-verb) to SVO (subject-verb-object). Example: G’o hanasjel orumu Tekai (“I speak the Tekoan language”).

Chapter IV: Verbs

Verbs conjugate for tense: present (-e), past (-u), future (-a), compound past (sji + -ok). Example with vaate (“to sit”):

PresentPastFutureCompound Past
g’o vaatelg’o vaatulg’o vaatalg’o sji vaatok
tan vaatentan vaatuntan vaatantan sji vaatok

Imperatives add -sje: mire (to see) → miresje! (Look!).
