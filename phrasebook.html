Tekoan Phrasebook

This phrasebook provides everyday expressions in Tekoan with their English meanings. It is designed for learners, travelers, and exam candidates to practice common greetings, introductions, and conversations.

Introducing Yourself

  • Gasay sji tanje g'orbesj? – What’s your name?
  • G'oje g'orbesj sji ____. – My name is ____.
  • G'o sji ____. – I’m ____.
  • Gafay sji tan? – How are you?
  • G'o sji sazbah, ve tan? – I’m fine, and you?
  • G'o sazbah bano. – I’m fine too.
  • Tan hanasjen Tekai? – Do you speak Tekoan?
  • Sai, g'o hanasjel Tekai. – Yes, I speak Tekoan.

Talking with a Friend

  • Akisa! Gafay sji tan? – Hello! How are you?
  • G'o sji sazbah, damsen, ve tan? – I’m fine, thanks, and you?
  • Imin sji sazbah. – All is good.
  • Gafay sji tanje bamisi? – How’s your family?
  • Manim sjim sazbah tofaz. – They are doing very well. My dad is a doctor and my mom is a teacher.
  • Sazbah tofaz! G'oje bamisi sji sazbah bano. – Very good! My family is also well. My dad is a professor and my mother was a linguist, now a housewife.
  • Ve tan, tan cjalen oyi sikken? – And you, do you work or study?
  • Saito, g'o sikkel Rukan Aziyan je orumukkan ve nanemakkan. – No, I study Eastasian Linguistics and Anthropology.
  • Emaru g'o cjalel ve sikkel. – Right now I’m working and studying. I am a police officer and also a student at the University of Muvan.

Talking with a Coworker

  • Huyi, Sadok… haben zjim kitnayim je zja esuntemisji? – Hey, Sadok… Do you have the project papers?
  • Sai, habel zjim. – Yes, I have them.
