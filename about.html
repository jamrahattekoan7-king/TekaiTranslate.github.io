About TekaiTranslate

TekaiTranslate is a modern translation platform dedicated to the Tekoan language (Tekai), the official language of the Kingdom of Bahnkut. It integrates linguistic resources, grammar guides, and exam preparation materials to help learners and researchers understand and use Tekai fluently.

About the National Board of Examiners

The National Board of Examiners of the Kingdom of Bahnkut is a governmental organization that manages the Kingdom's policies and standardization on matters of administration and civic regulations. In the field of Tekoan, the PBE (per Tekoan initials) is tasked with developing, updating, and regulating the Tekoan Language Exam (EOT), which is the standard Tekoan language exam offered to foreigners and Bahnkutians alike.

Exam Levels Explained

Every EOT measures four distinct linguistic elements: reading, writing, listening, and speaking. The three EOT levels correspond with the CEFR language levels: A1–A2 (Beginner), B1–B2 (Intermediate), and C1–C2 (Advanced). Each exam lasts 2 hours and 15 minutes regardless of level, with differences in vocabulary, grammar exercises, and technical terms for intermediate and advanced candidates.

Exam Schedule

  • First Quarter – January 15–17
  • Second Quarter – April 15–17
  • Third Quarter – July 15–17
  • Fourth Quarter – October 15–17

The exam runs over three days: EOT‑01 on the 15th, EOT‑02 on the 16th, and EOT‑03 on the 17th. According to the Bahnkutian Constitution, foreigners applying for citizenship must achieve EOT‑03 (Advanced) proficiency. Certificates expire after five years, ensuring continued mastery of the Tekoan language, our national heritage.

About the Tekoan Language

Brief History

King Haran Prasjad I of the Kingdom of Bahnkut developed the Tekoan language in AD 1370. It is the native language of the Kingdom and is spoken by 97% of Bahnkutians. Over the centuries, it has undergone many changes and is currently written in Latin, Marsjala, Acjnahin, and Arabic scripts. Today, it serves as the language of civil and governmental spaces.

Standard Dialect

The Standard Tekoan Dialect (Haranic Tekoan) originates from Lans‑ha‑Haran district, whose capital is Haran City. Historically, it was first written using the Marsjala script, and in 1957 the Latin script was added and made the official standard. Many Bahnkutians still use Marsjala for household and religious purposes, and it remains a patriotic symbol of Bahnkutian identity, appearing in advertising and government logos.

The New Standard Tekoan-English Dictionary

The Inesin Sestander Tekai‑Yengai Bujan (New Standard Tekoan‑English Dictionary) is an official Bahnkut Government project to record, promote, and teach the Tekoan language. With over 250 entries, it is designed to help learners achieve fluency and enjoy the privileges of living in Bahnkut. The dictionary includes an accessible grammar section and was compiled by Prof. Dr. Markan Sjefahan as part of his Bahnkutian Linguistics project at the University of Lans‑ha‑Haran.
