Languages

The translator works primarily with the Tekoan language, the native tongue of the Kingdom of Bahnkut, alongside supporting languages for accessibility and comparison. Each language page provides resources tailored to learners and researchers.

Tekoan

Spoken by 97% of Bahnkutians, Tekoan is the national language and cultural heritage of Bahnkut. It is written in multiple scripts: Latin (standard), Marsjala (traditional), Acjnahin, and Arabic. The Tekoan page offers grammar guides, phrasebooks, and exam preparation materials.

Spanish

Spanish is used as a bridge language for many learners outside Bahnkut. The Spanish page provides comparative translations, bilingual phrasebooks, and grammar notes to help Spanish speakers acquire Tekoan more easily.

English

English serves as the global reference language. The English page includes the New Standard Tekoan-English Dictionary, exam explanations, and cultural notes to make Tekoan accessible to international learners.

How Each Page Works

  • About: Background on Bahnkut, the National Board of Examiners, and the EOT exam.
  • Languages: Overview of Tekoan and supporting languages, with dedicated resources per language.
  • Grammar: Detailed explanations of Tekoan grammar rules, conjugations, and syntax changes.
  • Phrasebook: Practical bilingual phrases (English–Tekoan) for everyday use.
